# manifesto
The current revenue model of the internet reduces culture to fit into 1-dimensional infinite feeds, forcing culture's creators to compete against each other for attention. There's been a rise in cultural attachment as the primary selling point for commerce, and thus creators push their fans and communities to consume products and services from brands via sponsorships. The vast majority of creators lose out on a large portion of value they create, or sell out their fans to unaligned companies that are only interested in profit. We believe in a world where kids aren't sold energy drinks by their heroes.


The reality of today is the average person is thrown into a system of mindless scrolling and consumption, constantly feeding a system that exploits them for profit. There's currently no way for consumers to get skin-in-the-game and meaningfully exert influence over the value they're generating. Instead, the majority of economic energy is captured by companies, and the people have no power to make changes they care about.


These brands earning off the back of culture are structurally disincentivized to make meaningful impact in improving subcultures they profit from. The majority of people are fed up with brands and companies that don't align with their values, but have no real alternative. Most feel brands make empty promises, and are only interested in profit, and at the same time most are willing to pay for alternatives that align with their view of the world. 


Creators and consumers are the ones that push culture forward, generate value and have meaningful visions for world change, but for-profit companies have been in the drivers seat, until now. The rise of token networks, Reddit subcultures, creator fanbases, DAOs, and other forms of community ownership are the first signs of a new paradigm. The internet is shifting from a place of mindless consumption to a place of meaningful communal participation, shared value creation, and social impact. 


We are building the Revolution Protocol for this new world order. Revolution empowers communities to collectively build and own their culture's future. The protocol enables the creation of decentralized brands, built and owned by the people, able to create positive change in the world on their own terms. It marks a paradigm shift in how people create shared value on the internet, and has the potential to flip our capitalist profit maximizing system on its head.


The product itself is a full stack economic engine for subcultural cultivation and social impact. Communities are empowered to come together, engage, and drive attention to the culture they're creating. Everyone in the community can earn money for themselves and a shared treasury by selling co-branded drops of goods, services, and NFTs. Crucially, any money earned for the community earns both the creator and supporter governance rights over the funds. The community can then collectively decide how to spend the money in the shared treasury, and can use it to fund projects, causes, and other initiatives.


Anyone is able to build on the brand. This permissionless innovation paired with governance emission to the people building and supporting the brand creates an incredibly powerful feedback loop for people to contribute to the growth of the brand. The more people contribute, the more the brand grows, the more money everyone earns, and the higher impact the brand can have in the world.


We call this the revolution cycle, and it's the future our cultures deserve.
